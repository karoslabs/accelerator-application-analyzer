# Techstars

## Base rate
Roughly 1% across most programs (confirmed by Techstars' own 2023 data). Acceptance rates vary slightly by city / vertical, but the bar is uniformly high. Around 10-12 companies per cohort per program.

## Stage fit
Sweet spot: post-prototype, pre-product-market-fit. Built a product, have a team, maybe some early traction, still tinkering with PMF and planning to keep raising. Techstars has funded companies from napkin-sketch to millions in ARR, but the classic accepted profile is scrappy team with early pull.

## Deal terms
$120K total ($20K for 6% common stock + $100K convertible note) in most programs.

## What Techstars actually wants (in their own words)
Official mantra: **"team, team, team, market, product, traction"** -- in that order.

Their four core selection criteria for founders:

1. **Coachability.** Non-negotiable. If partners think you won't take feedback, you're out. This matters more at Techstars than at most accelerators because the program is mentor-driven.
2. **Execution.** Work hard and work smart. Prioritize and delegate. Show bias for action.
3. **Prior history.** Have the co-founders stuck together through adversity? Shipped things before? Done the hard thing?
4. **Passion / commitment.** Are you in this for the long haul?

## What makes a strong Techstars application
- A strong team video (Techstars weighs video heavily -- keep it under a minute, founder-led, no corporate voiceover)
- A clear "unfair advantage" -- why *you*, specifically
- Clear story of how the specific Techstars program (city, vertical, partners) helps you. They want you to treat them as a potential co-founder, not a generic check.
- Honest about weaknesses. Partners hate founders who pretend they have no gaps.
- A north-star metric of monthly trackable growth
- 1-4 hours of real effort on the written application (it shows)

## Common reasons to get rejected
- Signals of un-coachability (ego, dismissing feedback in interviews, "we already know")
- Co-founder friction visible in the interview
- No clear reason why *this* Techstars program vs any other
- Generic video with corporate narration
- Submitting applications to multiple Techstars programs thinking it increases odds (it doesn't -- they share applications)
- Hiding weaknesses rather than naming them

## Interview format
First round: two back-to-back 15-minute interviews with different team members. Second round: screening committee with multiple Techstars people plus outside experts for niche verticals. Some locations (NYC) add an onsite whiteboard session.

## The founder archetype that wins
Visible humility without being a pushover. Has shipped things before. Clear why this co-founding team is durable. Specific about which Techstars program and why. Can name the mentors or partners in that program they want to work with. Owns the gaps in the team and has a plan to fill them.

## Scoring weights for this program
- Team (coachability + execution + history): 45%
- Product: 15%
- Traction: 10%
- Market: 10%
- Founder-program fit (why this specific program): 15%
- Application craft (esp. video): 5%

## Red flag multipliers
- Any signal of un-coachability: x 0.2
- Applied to 3+ Techstars programs simultaneously without picking a favorite: x 0.6
- No video or corporate-style video: x 0.5
- Generic "Techstars would help us grow" answer: x 0.7

## Green flag multipliers
- Named a specific Techstars mentor or partner in the application: x 1.5
- Techstars alum intro / referral: x 2
- Geographic or vertical fit with the specific program is obvious: x 1.5
- Prior "done hard things together" story for the founding team: x 1.5
