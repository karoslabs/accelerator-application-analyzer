# Y Combinator

## Base rate
Roughly 1% overall. Recent batches have dipped to 0.6% (Summer 2025 hit the lowest on record). YC now runs 4 batches a year with 140-200 companies per cohort. Application volume per cycle: 20,000-40,000+.

Important nuance: the raw rate is misleading. Thousands of applications are dead on arrival (solo non-technical founders building complex software, weak writing, no market understanding). The "real" rate for a serious, well-crafted application is closer to 5-15%.

At the interview stage, 20-30% of interviewed teams get in. The written application is the dominant filter.

## Stage fit
Any stage from idea to revenue. YC openly says "some of you are here despite your idea." But recent batches have been ~88% AI-native and ~84% B2B. Consumer is swimming upstream. International founders are welcome but must relocate to SF for 11 weeks.

## Deal terms
$500K total: $125K for 7% post-money SAFE, plus $375K on uncapped MFN SAFE.

## What YC actually wants (in priority order)
1. **Team.** YC bets on founders, not ideas. Specifically:
   - At least one technical co-founder who can ship the product. Outsourcing core product = major red flag.
   - Co-founders who have worked together before or built things together. A history of "making things happen."
   - Domain insight. Why are *you* the right people for this problem?
   - Tenacity. "Founders are cockroaches, not unicorns."
   - Equity splits near 50/50 for 2 founders. Splits like 80/20 raise red flags.
2. **Insight + product.** Something people want. Clear, specific, non-obvious. No jargon.
3. **Traction (nice to have, not required).** At idea stage YC still funds. But if you have any pull -- users, revenue, retention -- lead with it.
4. **Market.** Big or becoming big. Explain *why now*.
5. **Written clarity.** Partners read hundreds of apps a day. If they cannot understand what you do in the first 30 seconds, you are out.

## Common reasons to get rejected
- Solo non-technical founder building software
- Outsourced dev team
- Jargon-heavy "we are an AI-powered platform for..." openers
- No clear user or buyer
- Vague or hypothetical traction
- Equity split signals one founder isn't really committed
- Consumer idea with no obvious retention loop

## The founder archetype that wins
Technical, has shipped before, clear co-founder relationship (worked together, balanced equity), can describe the product in one sentence a non-expert understands, has something live in the world (even if small), explains *why this team for this problem* in one line.

## Scoring weights for this program
- Team: 35%
- Product / insight: 25%
- Traction: 15%
- Market: 10%
- Founder-program fit: 5%
- Application craft: 10%

## Red flag multipliers (apply these on top of the base multiplier)
- Solo non-technical: x 0.3
- Outsourced tech: x 0.4
- No user or customer in 6+ months of building: x 0.6
- Consumer without retention data: x 0.7
- First-time founder, no prior shipping track record: x 0.8

## Green flag multipliers
- Prior YC founder or exit: x 3-5
- Working at a top-tier company in the relevant domain + left to build this: x 1.5-2
- Fast growth (20%+ week over week): x 2-4
- Revenue at application time: x 1.5-2.5
