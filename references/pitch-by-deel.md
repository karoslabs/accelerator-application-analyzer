# The Pitch by Deel

## Base rate
**0.05% final selection rate** (per Deel’s own announcement) — more selective on paper than most accelerators. Over 20,000 startups expected to apply across 7 global regions.

But the structure makes the math more nuanced:
- ~100 regional winners receive a $50,000 SAFE
- 10 global champions receive a $1,000,000 SAFE
- Total prize pool: $15M

For probability estimation: treat the **regional winner base rate at ~0.5%** and the **global winner base rate at ~0.05%**. When the founder asks about their odds, default to regional unless they explicitly ask about global.

## Stage fit
Pre-seed, seed, sometimes early Series A. Typically have raised under $3M (some sources say $5M). Must have an MVP. Sector-agnostic. Open to founders worldwide.

## Deal terms
$50K SAFE (regional winners) or $1M SAFE (global winners). Founder-friendly: Deel explicitly says they don’t want large ownership stakes and won’t compete with lead investors. They can join existing rounds. Standard information rights, terms negotiable.

## Format
1. Apply online (5-min application + short pitch — video not mandatory but helps)
2. AI-powered scoring + expert human judges review
3. If selected as Golden Tier, invited to a regional final
4. Pitch live: 2-minute live presentation with supporting slides
5. Regional winners get $50K and travel to the global finale
6. Global finale (May 2026) — 10 winners x $1M SAFE

Regional finals: Tel Aviv, Dubai, Singapore, New York, Paris, London, Berlin.

## What The Pitch by Deel actually wants
Per their own published criteria, evaluation is across:

1. **Product strength.** Working MVP, clear differentiation.
2. **Market opportunity.** Big enough to matter, real demand evidence.
3. **Team capability.** Especially "borderless founders" — globally-minded teams.
4. **Traction metrics.** Specific numbers, not vague claims.
5. **Scalability potential.** Can this be a global business, not just a local one.

The judging is explicitly designed to surface "high-signal" startups across geographies — Deel built this to reward founders outside traditional VC corridors. Founders from Latin America, MENA, SE Asia, Africa, Eastern Europe are not just welcome, they’re a strategic priority.

## What makes a strong Pitch application
- **Clarity in 2 minutes.** The format rewards founders who have done the work of distilling. If you can’t explain it in 2 minutes, it’s diagnostic — fix the story before applying.
- **A clean 10–12 slide deck.** Problem (quantified in dollars or hours), solution, traction, team, ask. The deck must stand alone.
- **Specific numbers.** Not "growing fast." Show MoM growth, revenue, retention, pipeline.
- **A global angle.** Even if you’re early, articulate how this becomes a global business. Deel is built around global hiring — they love founders who think borderless.
- **A non-obvious geography is a feature, not a bug.** Founders in São Paulo, Lagos, Jakarta, Tel Aviv have a structural advantage in this competition relative to founders in SF.

## Common reasons to get rejected
- Pitch that runs past 2 minutes or buries the lead
- Vague traction
- Localized vision with no global scaling story
- Pure ideation with no MVP
- Generic "we use AI to..." framing
- Deck that doesn’t read on its own

## The founder archetype that wins
Builds globally from day one. Has an MVP and real traction numbers. Can pitch the company in 90 seconds. Sits in a region outside the traditional VC corridor but has a global thesis. Already raised some pre-seed or seed but not capped out yet. Comfortable on stage.

## Scoring weights for this program
- Product + clarity: 25%
- Traction: 20%
- Team (borderless / global thesis): 20%
- Market + scalability: 20%
- Pitch craft (the 2-min story + the deck): 15%

## Red flag multipliers
- Cannot articulate the company in 2 minutes: × 0.3
- No MVP: × 0.3
- Vague traction with no real numbers: × 0.5
- Hyper-local product with no global thesis: × 0.6
- Already raised >$5M: × 0.5

## Green flag multipliers
- Strong founder presence on stage / on camera: × 1.5
- Real traction with specific MoM numbers: × 2
- Founder from underrepresented geography with global ambition: × 1.5–2
- Existing institutional check (a16z, Ribbit, Stripe portfolio) or warm intro: × 2
- Crisp 10-slide deck that reads on its own: × 1.3
