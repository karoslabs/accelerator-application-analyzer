# a16z Speedrun

## Base rate
**0.4% in the most recent reported cohort.** 19,000+ applications, 50-70 startups accepted per cohort. Two cohorts a year. The hardest accelerator door to open right now.

Of applicants who reach the live video interview, about 10% are interviewed. After interview the conversion is meaningfully higher.

## Stage fit
Pre-seed / seed. Working prototype required. Solo founders accepted. Technical co-founder not strictly required but technical depth helps. Started as a games + entertainment focus, now horizontal -- AI, gaming, entertainment, consumer, frontier tech. Must relocate to San Francisco for 12 weeks (in-person, immersive).

## Deal terms
Up to $1,000,000. Typical: $500K up front for 10% via SAFE, plus $500K available if you raise your next round within 18 months at outside investor terms. Equity-expensive vs YC, but the program is also structurally heavier in operator support.

## What Speedrun actually wants
The program backs **founders who are already executing**, not founders who are still searching for an idea. Specifically:

1. **Velocity.** The whole brand is "speed." Show that you ship fast, learn fast, iterate fast. Founders who win at Speedrun are the ones who post product updates faster than their VCs can read them.
2. **Founder-market fit.** Why *you*, in detail. Personal story, prior domain experience, scar tissue from working in this space. Speedrun GM Joshua Lu specifically said the strongest applications "treat the application like an internal strategy memo, not a pitch."
3. **Evidence over adjectives.** Working prototype, demo in circulation, early users, customer conversations that prove demand. "Small spark" worth pouring fuel on.
4. **Bold vision.** A clear 5-10 year picture of what this becomes. Speedrun explicitly does not want incremental products.
5. **Greed for the network.** The accepted founders who get the most out of Speedrun are the ones who aggressively use a16z's 600-person operator team. The program rewards that energy in the application -- show specifically what you'd ask for help with in the first 90 days.

## What makes a strong Speedrun application
- Treat the application as a strategy memo, not a pitch. State the real problem, why it's structurally hard, why your team is positioned to solve it, what's working, what isn't, and what specific help you need.
- Foreground founder-market fit before market theory.
- Clear evidence: weekly active users, retention, conversion, time to value.
- Use AI to fix grammar and structure, but keep your authentic voice. Speedrun explicitly tells founders to do this.
- Be honest about risks and unknowns. Precision and candor signal maturity.

## Common reasons to get rejected
- Pitching an idea you researched last month vs lived in
- Market-theory monologues with no evidence
- Buzzwords ("AI-powered, blockchain-enabled, agentic")
- No working prototype
- Unclear team dynamics or capability gaps
- Founders who can't articulate what they need help with
- Founders unwilling to relocate to SF for 12 weeks

## Interview format
Live video, ~30 min, 2-3 a16z investors. Deep dives on product architecture, data strategy, distribution, and the *sequencing* of milestones. They want to see you make crisp trade-offs under time pressure.

## The founder archetype that wins
Has shipped a working prototype. Has evidence of pull, even if small. Can describe the product in one paragraph that a smart 12-year-old understands. Knows their numbers cold. Has a story for why they personally are the right person. Has a list of 3 things they'd ask the operator team for help with on day one. Is willing to live in SF and grind for 12 weeks.

## Scoring weights for this program
- Team + founder-market fit: 30%
- Velocity / shipping evidence: 25%
- Product / insight: 20%
- Traction: 10%
- Vision (boldness + clarity): 10%
- Application craft (memo style): 5%

## Red flag multipliers
- No prototype: x 0.2
- Market-theory framing with no evidence: x 0.4
- Cannot or will not relocate: x 0.5
- Generic AI / agent / platform language: x 0.5
- No clear founder-market fit story: x 0.5

## Green flag multipliers
- Existing a16z portfolio intro: x 3
- Demonstrably fast shipping cadence (e.g. weekly updates, public changelog): x 2
- Revenue or strong retention: x 2-3
- Prior exit or repeat founder: x 3
- Novel insight in AI / consumer / games / entertainment: x 1.5-2.5
