# 500 Global (Flagship Accelerator)

## Base rate
Roughly 1–1.5% for the Flagship program. Historically 500 has cited "less than 3%" but more recent data points put it around 1%. Rolling admissions (no fixed cycle), reviewed year-round. Backed companies include Canva, Grab, Credit Karma.

## Stage fit
Pre-seed / seed. Early-stage tech, ideally with an MVP and at least early signals of demand. International founders are explicitly welcomed — 20–30% of accepted companies are based outside the US. Must be able to relocate to Palo Alto for the 4-month in-person program.

## Deal terms
$150,000 seed investment for 6% equity stake. There’s also a program fee deducted from the investment (covers Palo Alto facility, mentors, programming) — ask about the current number when applying.

## What 500 Global actually wants
500 evaluates founders across **six core dimensions**:

1. **Team capability + founder–market fit.** Can you execute? Do you have resilience, coachability, and crucially, **a sense of urgency**?
2. **Co-founder alignment.** How do you handle adversity and disagreement? Co-founder dynamics are scrutinized.
3. **Traction.** Even at early stage, "traction" is expected — revenue, users, pilots, deep product progress. They will push hard on specific numbers.
4. **Product / insight.** Is what you’re building meaningfully different?
5. **Market.** Big enough and reachable.
6. **Global scalability.** 500 is explicitly global. They invest in ~30 countries. Bonus points if you have a clear plan for international expansion.

## What makes a strong 500 Global application
- Know your numbers cold. Founders consistently report being asked for granular metrics (growth by month, cohort retention, CAC, conversion by channel) they hadn’t prepared for.
- Hyper-prepared but authentic — practiced answers that don’t sound rehearsed.
- A clear "unfair advantage" — why your team specifically wins this market.
- Sense of urgency. 500 specifically calls this out as a signal they look for. Founders who feel like they’re already running win.
- A real international growth angle if you have one.

## Common reasons to get rejected
- Vague metrics or "we’ll figure it out" answers when pressed on numbers
- Co-founder dynamics that look fragile under pressure
- No sense of urgency — founders who treat the interview like a casual chat
- Localized vision with no path to scale beyond home market
- "Just an idea" — 500 wants to see motion

## Interview format
Application → review (a few weeks turnaround) → interview round → decision. Expect rapid-fire interviews where the panel pivots between strategy, numbers, and founder DNA. They reward founders who can hold all three contexts at once.

## The founder archetype that wins
Has shipped, has numbers, has urgency, has a plan to go global, can answer "what do you do when your co-founder disagrees with you" with a real example, treats every question like a chance to show motion. Often international founders looking at the US market through 500 as their bridge.

## Scoring weights for this program
- Team + co-founder dynamics: 25%
- Founder–market fit + urgency: 20%
- Traction + numbers literacy: 25%
- Product: 10%
- Market + global scalability: 15%
- Application craft: 5%

## Red flag multipliers
- Cannot quote specific growth or retention numbers: × 0.4
- Co-founder dynamic feels fragile: × 0.5
- Hyper-local vision with no global thesis: × 0.7
- Calm energy where urgency should be: × 0.6

## Green flag multipliers
- Ex-operator at a notable global startup: × 1.5–2
- Strong international expansion thesis already in motion: × 1.5
- Already raised some institutional money but want the network: × 1.3
- Founders from underrepresented geographies with traction: × 1.5–2 (500 actively looks for this)
- Revenue + clear unit economics: × 2
